# Topical Cream Architecture for CMLase-ECM

## Design Objective
Preserve enzyme activity during storage and application while maximizing ECM access in skin-relevant conditions.

For a chemistry-and-module rationale focused on CrGO-derived CMLase concepts, see docs/conceptual_cmlase_formulation.md.

## 1. Functional Architecture
A practical architecture is a two-phase system with controlled activation at point of use:

- Phase A (enzyme microenvironment):
  - Buffers and stabilizers tuned for enzyme integrity.
  - Optional protective encapsulation for oxidation/moisture stress reduction.
- Phase B (dermal vehicle):
  - Emulsion or gel-cream base optimized for spreadability and skin contact.
  - Excipients selected for compatibility with enzyme activity.

Point-of-use mixing reduces long-term incompatibility between enzyme and reactive excipients.

## 2. Component Logic

### Enzyme Stabilization Layer
- Maintain local pH band supporting retained activity.
- Include polyol/sugar alcohol or protein-compatible stabilizers when compatible with catalysis.
- Minimize surfactant exposure that denatures protein structure.

### Vehicle Layer
- Favor mild, non-denaturing emulsifier systems.
- Keep ionic strength and preservative strategy compatible with enzyme viability.
- Use oxygen/peroxide management if oxidase turnover generates reactive species concern.

### Optional ECM-Targeting Layer
- Candidate options:
  - Collagen-binding peptide tags.
  - Reversible matrix-affinity motifs.
- Goal:
  - Increase residence time in superficial dermal ECM while limiting broad diffusion.

## 3. Delivery Constraints in Skin
- Stratum corneum limits penetration of large proteins.
- Practical strategy is to optimize activity near accessible ECM interfaces and appendages while balancing safety.
- Supportive options may include:
  - Hydration-mediated barrier softening.
  - Mild penetration-assist approaches compatible with protein stability.

## 4. Concrete Formulation Template (Example Framework)

## Product Concept
- Format: dual-chamber pump (enzyme concentrate + vehicle base).
- Use: mixed immediately before application.

## Chamber A: Enzyme Concentrate
- CMLase-ECM lead or lead blend.
- Protein-compatible buffering and stabilizing agents.
- Packaging: oxygen-limiting and light-protective.

## Chamber B: Vehicle Base
- Mild emulsion base with dermatology-friendly sensorial profile.
- Excipient set pre-screened against activity-retention panel.
- Preservative system selected for protein compatibility.

## Mixed Product Performance Targets
- Homogeneous mixing within seconds.
- Application pH in skin-compatible mildly acidic-to-near-neutral window.
- Prespecified retained activity over realistic on-skin exposure interval.

## 5. Stability Program
Track at minimum:
- Refrigerated and ambient storage trends.
- Freeze-thaw sensitivity.
- Agitation/shear sensitivity during dispensing.
- Activity retention after repeated opening/use simulations.

Primary outputs:
- Residual activity curve.
- Physical stability status.
- Acceptability decision by predefined thresholds.

## 6. Safety-by-Design Notes
- Prioritize specificity controls to reduce off-target protein chemistry.
- Treat co-formulated matrix remodeling agents as separate modules with independent risk controls.
- Keep proteolytic and oxidase-active systems functionally separated unless validated for compatibility and safety.
